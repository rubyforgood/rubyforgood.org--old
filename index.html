About Ruby for Good

Ruby for Good is an annual event based out of the DC-metro area where Ruby programmers from all over the globe get together for a long weekend to build projects that help our communities. In 2016 the event will take place in Front Royal, VA at George Mason University’s Smithsonian-Mason School of Conservation. Participants stay in on-site dorms; hacking and socializing takes place in communal areas. Join us just for the fun of giving back! Questions? Check out the FAQ or drop us a note.

Down to Business

Projects in 2016 are focused around conservation and the environment. We are extremely excited to announce that our first confirmed project is for researchers working with red pandas. That's right, RED FRICKIN’ PANDAS. Here is a primer on their cuteness. But red pandas won’t be all... we need YOU to help us find other great projects - suggest a project below! Project details, including available teams to join, will be announced to attendees around the beginning of June. Team leads will get information on projects in mid-to-late April.

Team Leads

Have you always thought to yourself "Self, I rock—and I should lead something!” Well here's your chance! We have lots of great projects and we need great people to lead them! Leading a team entails connecting with stakeholders, initial architecting, planning out project milestones, and keeping your team moving forward. Indicate your willingness when you register and we’ll be in touch!

On a related note, we couldn’t successfully organize Ruby for Good without amazing volunteer team leads. Once again a huge thanks for all the hard work put in by last year’s leads: Jim Gay, Betsy Haibel, Adam Walker, Brandon Rice, Aaron Kromer, Jason Wieringa, Ali Ibrahim, Noah Patterson, and Robert Winter.

What you need to know

Bring yourself, your laptop, and whatever you plan on wearing. Lodging consists of shared dorm rooms (what better way to get to know new people!) complete with linens, pillows, blankets and towels. We'll also be supplying food, drinks, and fun atmosphere. For a more in-depth run down of the weekend, check out our More Info page. More questions? Take a gander through the FAQ or drop us a note.

What does it cost?

$200 all-inclusive. Registration includes room, linens, pillows, towels, swag, and three all-you-can-eat meals a day. We are a break-even operation; all funds go toward paying for the event!

Anything else?

You’ll spend dinner meeting with your team, chatting about your projects, and getting your environments aligned. We’ve found the event is most successful, with the most buy-in, when teams are a cohesive bunch from the beginning.
