About Ruby for Good

Ruby for Good is an annual event based out of the DC-metro area where Ruby programmers from all over the globe get together over a weekend to build projects to help our communities. The conference will take place at Smithsonian Mason Conservation Institute with the participants staying in the dorms and brainstorming, hacking, and cooking together in communal areas. We'll be working on projects that help our communities. We do this in our free time just for the fun of giving back! If you have any questions please drop us a note.

Down to Business

We are extremely excited to announce that our first confirmed project for next year is for researchers working with red pandas. That's right, RED FRICKIN’ PANDAS. Here is a primer on their cuteness. But red pandas won’t be all… we need YOU to help us find other great projects!

Last year we had a lot of great feedback about finding amazing, locally-based organizations to help. We are again relying on you and your networks to make suggestions! We will gather projects until late March and have final selections by the time registration opens in April. People who bring projects will get first crack at leading a team. Team Leads

Have you always thought to yourself "Self, I rock—and the world will know. And the world will learn. And the world will wonder how you make the tables turn.” Well, wait no longer. We will have lots of great projects and we need great people to lead them! Leading a team entails connecting with stakeholders, initial architecting, planning out project milestones, and keeping your team moving forward. Indicate your willingness when you register and we’ll be in touch!

On a related note, we couldn’t successfully organize Ruby for Good without amazing volunteer team leads. Once again a huge thanks for all the hard work put in by last year’s leads: Jim Gay, Betsy Haibel, Adam Walker, Brandon Rice, Aaron Kromer, Jason Wieringa, Ali Ibrahim, Noah Patterson, and Robert Winter.

What you need to know

You just need to bring yourself, your laptop, and whatever you plan on wearing. You will get your own dorm room complete with linens, pillow, blanket and towels so you don't have to worry about hauling that stuff with you. We'll also be supplying food, drinks, and fun atmosphere. If you have a particular board game or card game that you would like to bring please do so... we can only guarantee that we'll have the equipment needed to play Artemis.

What does it cost?

We hope to keep tickets to $150 all-inclusive; but that depends on our awesome sponsors offsetting the cost of registration. Registration includes room, board, linens, pillows, towels, swag, and all food (contact us with any dietary restrictions).

Anything else?

The conference runs from June 16st through June 19th, it is pretty important that everyone be there at the kickoff on the morning of the 16th. If there are extenuating circumstances we can certinally try to work with you, but this is the time where teams are picked and projects are started&emdash;and critical to having a successful (and engaguing) weekend.
